# AI Contribution Policy

This page defines the minimum review, provenance, and accountability standard for AI-assisted contributions to YAi!.

AI-assisted contributions are allowed, but the human contributor remains fully responsible for the contribution.

By submitting a contribution, the contributor certifies that:

1. they have reviewed and understood the submitted code, documentation, or asset;
2. they have the right to submit it under the project license and contribution terms;
3. the contribution does not knowingly copy proprietary, confidential, or incompatible code;
4. the contribution does not include secrets, credentials, private endpoints, customer data, or private business information;
5. AI-generated or AI-assisted code has been tested and integrated by the contributor;
6. the contributor can explain the design and implementation choices in the pull request.

For substantial AI-assisted contributions, the maintainer may request:

- a design explanation;
- test coverage;
- provenance notes;
- rewrite of unclear or overgenerated code;
- confirmation that no incompatible source was intentionally copied.

Low-effort, unclear, unreviewed, overgenerated, or provenance-ambiguous AI-generated pull requests may be closed without review.

AI tools are not contributors, copyright holders, maintainers, or legal authors of this project. The human contributor remains responsible for the submitted work.
